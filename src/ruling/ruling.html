About Social Security Tax

The current criteria for determining the Social Security Tax is as follows:

  • Get the Tax Base for the previous year (in this case {{$ctrl.year - 1}}) - which is Previous Year Gross Income * {{$ctrl.nominalExpenseRates[$ctrl.year-1]}}
  • To that, add all the Social Tax paid in the previous year.
  • Subtract 25% of that sum.
  • This is now the basis for calculating the Social Tax for year {{$ctrl.year}}. We multiply this base with 0.382 (38.2%) to get the total Yearly Social Tax to be paid this year.
  • However, this tax has a minimum and maximum, so it cannot be lower than 4264.32€/year or higher than 25424.52€/year as of {{$ctrl.year}}.
